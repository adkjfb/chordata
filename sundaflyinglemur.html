Sunda Flying Lemur

Lifespan: Up to 15 years in captivity, unknown in the wild

Top speed: Unknown

Weight: 2-4.5 lbs

Diet: Soft plant parts, including leaves, fruit, buds, and nectar

Predators: Snakes and birds of prey

Range: Southeast Asia

Conservation Status: Least concern

Fun Fact: The flap of skin connecting the arms, legs, and neck of the Sunda flying lemur is called the patagium.


Fun Fact: Flying lemurs have bottom incisors that are shaped like combs, which they use for grooming and to remove parasites.
