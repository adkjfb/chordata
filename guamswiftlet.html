Mariana Swiftlet

Lifespan: 12-15 years

Top speed: Not documented, but common swifts can fly up to 70 mph

Weight: 6-9 grams

Diet: Flying insects, including bees, wasps, beetles, and ants

Predators: Invasive species such as cats and brown tree snakes, potentially the native coconut crab

Range: Guam, northern Mariana islands

Conservation Status: Vulnerable

Fun Fact: Mariana swiftlets only lay one egg at a time.


Fun Fact: Mariana swiflets roost in caves and use echolocation to navigate them.
