Plainfin Midshipman

Lifespan: Unknown

Top speed: Unknown

Weight: 10-50 grams for females, type I males are 30-200 grams and type II males are 4-18 grams

Diet: Crustaceans, fish, frequently other plainfin midshipman, especially juveniles

Predators: Gulls, herons, and other birds, larger fish

Range: Eastern Pacific Ocean

Conservation Status: Least concern

Fun Fact: Plainfin midshipman have two kinds of males. Type I males claim a nest that females lay eggs in, and are about 9 times more common than Type II males. He then guards the nest until the eggs hatch. Type II males are significantly smaller and fertilize eggs in the nests of Type I males.


Fun Fact: Plainfin midshipman have photophores that allow them to bioluminesce. Only the southern population does this, and only during mating season. Unlike the southern population, the northern population does not have a chemical called luciferin in their photophores. When this chemical is injected, northern midshipmen glow as well.
