Philippine Flying Lemur

Lifespan: Unknown

Top speed: 22 mph when gliding

Weight: 2-4 lbs

Diet: Mostly young leaves, also fruits and buds

Predators: Philippine eagle, snakes such as pythons

Range: Southern Philippines

Conservation Status: Least concern

Fun Fact: Also called colugos, flying lemurs do not fly, though they ar excellent gliders and can glide upwards of 100 meters. They are also not lemurs, or even primates.


Fun Fact: Philippine flying lemurs obtain most of their water by licking it from leaves and from the water in the leaves and fruit they eat.
