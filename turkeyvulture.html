Turkey Vulture

Lifespan: 16 years in the wild, up to 30 in captivity

Weight: 3-6 lbs

Diet: Almost entirely carrion

Predators: Fledglings are eaten by birds of prey, including golden eagles, bald eagles, and great horned owl

Range: Southern Canada to the tip of South America

Conservation Status: Least concern

Fun Fact: Turkey vultures can smell carrion from over a mile away, and travel up to 200 miles per day.


Fun Fact: Turkey vultures are highly resistant to many diseases found on animal carcasses, including anthrax, rabies, and botulism.
