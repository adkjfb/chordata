Wood Duck

Lifespan: Up to 20 years, though most do not live past 4 years. The oldest recorded wood duck was 23 years old.

Top speed: 30-35 mph in flight

Weight: 1-2 lbs

Diet: Invertebrates, aquatic plants, and seeds. Acorns are wood ducks' primary food source in the winter

Predators: Owls, raccoons, snapping turtles, minks, black rat snakes, red foxes,

Range: United States, southern Canada, northern Mexico

Conservation Status: Least concern

Fun Fact: The name "wood duck" comes from the fact that wood ducks are one of the few species of waterfowl that nest in tree cavities.


Fun Fact: The wood duck's scientific name, Aix sponsa, means bridal waterfowl, referring to the male's colorful crest.
