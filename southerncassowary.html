Double Wattled Cassowary

Lifespan: 18-20 years in the wild, up to 40 in captivity

Top speed: 30 mph

Weight: 100-150 lbs

Diet: Mostly fruit and seeds, also small vertebrates and insects

Predators: Dingoes, quolls, crocodiles, New Guinea singing dogs, monitor lizards, and pythons will eat young cassowaries or eggs, but adults are rarely preyed upon

Range: Rainforests of Papau New Quinea and some of northern Australia

Conservation Status: Vulnerable

Fun Fact: Despite being commonly recognized as the world's most dangerous bird, cassowaries have only killed two people in recorded history. One was an Australian teenager who had hit the cassowary with a bad, and the other was a man in Florida who tried to keep a captive cassowary.


Fun Fact: A cassowary's helmet is thought to be used to release heat, similar to a person sweating.
